Motivation

"It's like Patatap but social and collaborative" was the idea that inspired this project. While the audio-visual experience that Patatap provides is neat and novel, we felt there was room for expansion. Patatap relies on the creativity of an individual to produce a result that is appealing. For those that aren't musically inclined, it serves mostly as a stylish soundboard.

We wanted something that was driven by more than just the individual, something that allows for a sort of collaborative creativity. Adding a social aspect was a start, but we quickly realized that technological limitations might mean that attempting to play sounds over a network could lead to synchronicity issues. Rather than trying to perfect the timings, we decided instead to embrace the potential desync and aim for a tool that is more about communication without the use of typical language over a tool that serves as an online music maker.

During ideation, the concept of creating "sound spaces" came up. These would essentially be the "rooms" where friends could come together to tap away and play sounds. We took the theme of "space" to the extreme, using it as the theme for the website and inspiring the visuals that would go with the sounds. As individuals participate, they could begin to light up a space. With the combined output of many individuals, the group could find an even brighter space with beautiful visuals. We hope that people can come together and interact with sound, and maybe find a pleasant tune unintentionally.

Authors

Matt Farejowicz and Joon Ko are both juniors at MIT who practice web development in their spare time. Matt is a Math with Computer Science major while Joon is a pure Computer Science major, but the two are united by their appreciation for music, quirky web apps, and big anime tiddies. Joon and Matt wanted to work together on a web.lab project to create something that could be highly interactive and fun for a small crowd.
